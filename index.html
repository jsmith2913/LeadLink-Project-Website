I. Executive Summary

Oak Valley Community Bank’s (OVCB) commercial lending lead generation and CRM preparation processes are largely manual and fragmented. The current Quest CRM implementation cannot download intent and contact data together and only tracks lead counts. An Excel spreadsheet is used as a middle layer between Quest CRM, RelPro, and Reonomy, introducing errors, consuming analyst time, and causing missed opportunities.

OVCB’s lead conversion rate is estimated at approximately 8%, and each lead takes 30–35 minutes to enter and configure properly. Analysts spend significant time manually combining data from Reonomy and RelPro, cleaning and reformatting it for Quest CRM, and drafting outreach emails.

LeadLink is a centralized, web‑based pre‑CRM application that eliminates the Excel middleman and integrates Reonomy and RelPro directly. Data is extracted into a Firebase database, transformed into Quest CRM‑compatible formats, and enriched with custom outreach emails generated via the ChatGPT API using a predefined template. Key features include:

  • Data integration across Reonomy and RelPro
  • Internal database as a secure, single source of truth
  • Automation of data preparation and CRM export
  • Email outreach automation per lead
  • Expanded CRM summaries and metrics

The proposed solution is expected to significantly increase efficiency and accuracy. With an estimated implementation cost of $5,000, LeadLink would save 8 hours per week for 2 analysts, yielding annual labor savings of about $37,400 and an estimated $15,000 in additional revenue from improved lead conversion. Security is enhanced by keeping data internal, and expanded metrics provide deeper insight into lead performance. The new system is projected to increase the lead conversion rate to around 12%.

By overhauling OVCB’s restricted and wasteful current system, LeadLink will enhance lead generation, automate data inputs, and generate summaries and emails for each lead—transforming OVCB’s process and substantially improving operational efficiency.

III. Business Analysis

Bank overview

Oak Valley Community Bank is a community‑focused financial institution operating 18 branches primarily in Northern California. As of December 31, 2024, Oak Valley Bancorp reported:

  • Total assets: $1.90 billion
  • Gross loans: $1.11 billion
  • Total deposits: $1.70 billion
  • Approximately 168 employees (4% workforce growth over the past year)

In Q4 2024, Oak Valley Bancorp achieved net income of $6.0 million, with total net income of $24.9 million for the year. The bank serves individuals, small to medium‑sized businesses, and the agricultural sector, offering personal and business banking, loans, mortgages, investment services, and specialized agricultural financing across California’s Central Valley and Sacramento Region.

OVCB is expanding its branch network, including a planned Lodi branch opening in mid‑2025, and is enhancing digital banking capabilities to improve customer experience and operational efficiency.

  • Strengths: Strong local presence, diverse product offerings, solid financial position
  • Weaknesses: Limited geographic reach, dependence on loan income, technology gaps
  • Opportunities: Technological advancements, expanded lending

Target business area

LeadLink focuses on the commercial lending department, specifically the lead generation process. The commercial lending department:

  • Provides loans and credit services to businesses
  • Assesses creditworthiness and structures/approves loans
  • Manages disbursement and repayment
  • Maintains long‑term client relationships and monitors risk

In the current system, approximately 30% of leads are abandoned due to delays in entry. Because banks earn most of their revenue through net interest margin (charging higher interest on loans than they pay on deposits), commercial lending is critical to profitability. Historically, lenders relied on referrals, walk‑ins, and advertising, but OVCB has invested in more active lead generation via software tools to maintain a competitive advantage during a strong growth phase.

End‑to‑end current workflow

Lead generation begins in two applications: Reonomy and RelPro. Both are B2B prospecting intelligence tools with different focuses:

  • Reonomy: Real estate lead generation
  • RelPro: Lead intent data

Leads are generated based on OVCB’s criteria and stored in each system. An employee then transfers lead information from both applications into a master Excel spreadsheet, combining data for the same leads and businesses and adding notes. The Excel sheet is manually formatted to fit Quest CRM and then uploaded.

From Quest CRM, senior staff select leads and perform additional research. Qualified leads are passed to lenders, who contact prospects and begin the lending process. This workflow takes about 10 minutes per lead, with the bank handling 25–30 new leads per week. Analysts spend 5–6 hours per week solely on formatting and data handling—time that could be redirected to qualifying and prioritizing leads. On average, it takes around two days for a lender to contact a lead.

V. Proposed Process Analysis

The proposed solution is a centralized, web‑based application that automates OVCB’s lead generation and CRM preparation. The application integrates directly with Reonomy and RelPro APIs, allowing users to search, retrieve, and manage leads in a single interface. It merges contact and intent data from RelPro with real estate data from Reonomy and stores leads in a Firebase database as a live, editable source of truth.

Users can browse, annotate, and selectively export leads in Quest CRM’s required format. For each lead, a custom outreach email is generated using the ChatGPT API and a predetermined template, eliminating 5–10 minutes of manual email drafting per lead. The backend handles CRM formatting, email generation, and data transfer, while the frontend provides an intuitive interface for search, review, export, and note entry. This structure reduces manual labor by an estimated 75–85%, minimizes human error, and ensures CRM‑readiness from the outset.

From manual to automated workflow

Currently, users log into Reonomy and RelPro separately, download CSVs, merge data in Excel, manually clean and format files for Quest CRM, and draft emails. This process consumes 12–15 analyst hours per week, with 1.5–2 minutes per lead spent on reformatting and 5 minutes per lead on email drafting. OVCB processes around 250 leads per month, resulting in roughly 21 hours per month lost to data formatting and email preparation alone.

Delays in entry cause up to 30% of leads to go cold or be lost entirely—about 75 missed opportunities per month. Assuming an average commercial loan size of $250,000 and a net interest margin of 3.35%, this equates to approximately $627,000 per year in missed loan revenue.

VI. Solution Assessment

LeadLink supports OVCB primarily by reducing the time and effort required for the initial stages of lead generation. As one stakeholder noted, “we get paid high salaries, but find ourselves spending a large chunk of our time on lead generation which any low‑level employee could do as well.” By replacing the master Excel sheet with an interactive dashboard, time is saved at multiple points in the process.

Automated data transfer addresses the largest pain point: manually entering data from one application to another without meaningful modification. With an estimated implementation cost of $5,000, LeadLink is projected to save 8 hours per week for 2 analysts, resulting in annual labor savings of $37,400 and an additional $15,000 in revenue from increased lead conversion.

Email generation further accelerates the process once leads reach lenders. A simple, consistent starting email shortens the time to first contact by several days, enabling high‑level employees to manage more leads. All employees involved in lead generation benefit by spending more time validating and prioritizing leads rather than simply moving them through the pipeline.

Critical success factors

  • Reliable data integration from Reonomy and RelPro
  • Automated CRM‑ready output in Quest’s required format
  • Effective email automation with minimal need for manual editing
  • User‑friendly UI for fast lead search and review
  • Clear time and cost savings to justify ROI

Feasibility analysis

Financial & operational feasibility
ROI > 400% Breakeven < 270 days

Financially, the system is attractive, with ROI exceeding 400% over five years and breakeven in under 270 days. Operationally, LeadLink fits within existing team roles, enhances current workflows, and does not require new hires. Adoption is expected to be straightforward, with minimal training.

Technical & schedule feasibility
Stable APIs 8‑week build

Technologically, LeadLink uses modern, proven tools with low risk: Reonomy and RelPro APIs, Firebase, and ChatGPT. The main risks involve email quality control and maintaining compatibility with Quest CRM. With tight scope and clear requirements, the system is schedule‑feasible, with only minor post‑launch refinements anticipated.

Stakeholders and impact

The primary beneficiaries are OVCB employees involved in lead generation and commercial lending. LeadLink saves time, improves data quality, and increases conversion, thereby reducing labor costs and increasing revenue. No job losses are anticipated; the system is positioned as an enhancement tool rather than a replacement for human roles. The only significant downside is the upfront implementation cost.

Implementation plan

OVCB should engage an entry‑level software developer with a strong backend focus. Because the application is internal, frontend UI requirements are modest, while backend robustness is critical. With a clear product requirements document (PRD), the system can be implemented in approximately 8 weeks, assuming:

  • Tight scope and avoidance of scope creep
  • Use of stable, well‑documented APIs (Reonomy, RelPro)
  • Fast, clear communication and feedback from OVCB during planning and testing

Indicative 8‑week timeline

  • Week 1: Finalize requirements and design system architecture
  • Weeks 2–3: Backend development, API integration, and Firebase setup
  • Weeks 4–5: Frontend UI for search, review, and notes
  • Week 6: ChatGPT email prompting and integration, CRM formatting logic
  • Week 7: Full system testing and feedback iteration
  • Week 8: Deployment, employee training, and short‑term refinements

Training requirements are modest, potentially consisting of short videos explaining the interface and features for current staff and future new hires. As a smaller organization, OVCB can maintain close collaboration with the developer throughout the project.

VII. Conclusion

The LeadLink system offers a highly feasible and impactful solution for OVCB’s commercial lending operations. By replacing a manual, Excel‑based lead generation workflow with a centralized, web‑based platform, the bank can realize substantial gains in efficiency, accuracy, and lead conversion.

Financially, LeadLink is compelling: a $5,000 implementation cost, annual labor savings of $37,400, and increased loan revenue of $15,000 support an ROI of over 400% within five years and breakeven in under 270 days. Technologically, the system leverages stable, well‑documented APIs (Reonomy, RelPro, ChatGPT, Firebase) and is designed for scalability and long‑term reliability.

Operationally, LeadLink enhances existing workflows without disrupting roles or requiring new hires. It empowers analysts, managers, and lenders by automating low‑value tasks and freeing time for strategic decision‑making. From a scheduling perspective, an 8‑week implementation timeline is realistic with clear scope and responsive collaboration.

Ultimately, LeadLink addresses core pain points in OVCB’s lead management process by reducing lead abandonment, cutting manual work by up to 85%, and enabling faster, more effective client outreach. It is a high‑impact investment aligned with OVCB’s strategic goals of growth, digital modernization, and increased profitability.
